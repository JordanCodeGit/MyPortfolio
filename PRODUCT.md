# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers scanning many portfolios in ~30 seconds, plus potential clients evaluating fit. Their context is fast triage: they arrive skeptical and time-poor, looking to answer "what does this person do, and is there proof?" before deciding whether to read further. A secondary, slower-reading pass (a hiring manager who's now interested) wants the decisions and measured outcomes behind the work.

## Product Purpose

A single-page personal portfolio for **Jordan Angkawijaya** — a Computer Science student and junior Project Manager who ships AI products (NLP, computer vision, full-stack). It exists to convert a 30-second scan into a memorable, credible impression: what he does, the products he's shipped, the outcomes they produced, and the leadership behind them.

Success is threefold: (1) a recruiter grasps the value proposition and sees verifiable proof within seconds; (2) the site is *remembered* — its botanical "nocturnal park" world and single signature scroll moment make it distinct from the flood of look-alike portfolios; (3) the restraint and precision themselves read as evidence of PM judgment.

## Brand Personality

**Calm · Precise · Distinctive.** The voice is honest, understated confidence — craft and clarity over noise. It should evoke quiet competence and taste: a person who makes deliberate choices, states things plainly, and doesn't oversell. Warmth comes from the world (the day/night park) and the writing, not from hype. Memorability is earned by real content and one bold gesture, not by decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / dev-tool landing template** — gradient hero, identical feature-card grid, the big-number "hero-metric" block. The AI-landing-page default; the single biggest tell to avoid.
- **Corporate agency stock** — stocky imagery, buzzword copy, safe navy-and-gray polish that says nothing specific.
- **Dense CV dump** — a cluttered wall of text and bullets that buries the strongest signals instead of ranking them.

(Note: loud, ambitious signature moments are *welcome* — the brief is not "quiet everywhere." The restraint is in the supporting sections so the one bold moment lands.)

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the proof.** For a PM, discipline and clarity are the credential. Every element must earn its place; cut anything that only decorates. The quiet supporting sections are what let the one bold moment read as intentional.
2. **Substance over animation.** Memorability comes from shipped products and measured outcomes, not effects. Motion is the skin; the work is the message. One well-orchestrated signature moment beats scattered micro-interactions.
3. **Reward the 30-second scan first, the slow read second.** Lead with the value proposition and verifiable proof; put depth on demand (case-study detail, "Verify ↗" links, sections) for the reader who chooses to go deeper.
4. **Honest framing is the brand.** Never inflate a role or invent a metric; precision reads as integrity (e.g. "3rd place Pilmapres," the EIT role described as shadowing, `[PLACEHOLDER]` over a guessed number). Verifiable links beat unverifiable claims.
5. **One signature moment carries memorability.** A single distinctive gesture — the Hero→Projects transition and the day/night park world — does the differentiating. Everything around it stays composed so it doesn't have to compete.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast (large/bold text ≥ 3:1) in both day and night themes; visible keyboard focus on every interactive element; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored throughout (final state rendered instantly, no pinned transition, no entrance animation). Semantic HTML, real `alt` text on every image, and honest, plain-language copy. Current build measures Lighthouse accessibility 100 on mobile — hold that line as sections are added.
